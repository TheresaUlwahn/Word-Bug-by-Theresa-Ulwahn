Presenting the word BUG

Meaning

A bug is a mistake or an error made in a program code ­or also in other tech­nical con­struc­tions and sys­tems. It can cause problems both by giving wrong results but also be­have un­ex­pec­tedly.

These problems are often fixed by the de­ve­lo­per, and nowa­days, new pro­grams have point up­dates to avoid or upgrade their launched pro­grams. That is called de­bugg­ing.

History

To add some history to it, Mr Thomas Edison himself, wrote a letter in 1878 re­gar­ding a prob­lem ("bugs") that occ­ured during his de­ve­lop­ment of the tele­graph sys­tem.

And in 1945 at Harvard University, a bug (literally one) was found in a elec­tronic calcu­lator, forcing it to crash. The bug which was attached to the log book is still there.
